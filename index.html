Faulty & Unsafe Products

You have the right to request a repair under the Consumer Guarantees Act (CGA) if a company provides you with a defective or dangerous consumer product. The shop may decide to replace, repair, or refund your money if a small defect in the goods is fixable.

Poor Quality or Incomplete Product

Poor quality and incomplete work are common consumer complaints in India's many industries. Customers frequently experience poor quality goods or services that fall short of their expectations.

Everyday Consumer Issues

Misleading and Incorrect Pricing

Misleading pricing is when a product is promoted in an unclear way, that may cause you to be misled about the true cost of the product. Incorrect pricing is when an advertised price is different from the actual price the seller intends to charge you for the product.

Repair Damage After Normal Use

When damage occurs to a product after normal use, consumers often face the challenge of seeking repairs or compensation

Right to Safety

Means the right to be protected against the marketing of goods and services, which are hazardous to life and property. The purchased goods and services availed of should not only meet their immediate needs, but also fulfil long term interests. Before purchasing, consumers should insist on the quality of the products as well as on the guarantee of the products and services

Right to be Informed

 Means right to be informed about the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard and price of goods so as to protect the consumer against unfair trade practices. Consumers should insist on getting all the information about the product or service before making a choice or a decision.

Read More ➔

Right to Choose

 Means right to be assured, wherever possible of access to a variety of goods and services at competitive price. In the case of monopolies, it means the right to be assured of satisfactory quality and service at a fair price. It also includes the right to basic goods and services. This is because the unrestricted right of the minority to choose can mean a denial for the majority of its fair share.

Read More ➔

Right to be Heard

Means that consumer's interests will receive due consideration at appropriate forums. It also includes the right to be represented in various forums formed to consider the consumer's welfare. The Consumers should form non-political and non-commercial consumer organizations which can be given representation in various committees formed by the Government and other bodies in matters relating to consumers.

Read More ➔

Right to Seek Approval

 Means right to seek redressal against unfair trade practices or unscrupulous exploitation of consumers. It also includes the right to fair settlement of the genuine grievances of the consumer. Consumers must make complaints for their genuine grievances. Many times their complaint may be of small value but its impact on the society as a whole may be very large.

Read More ➔

Right to Consumer Education

Means the right to acquire the knowledge and skill to be an informed consumer throughout life. Ignorance of consumers, particularly of rural consumers, is mainly responsible for their exploitation. They should know their rights and must exercise them. Only then real consumer protection can be achieved with success

Consumer Challenges faced in India

Consumerism is still in its infancy and that’s why majority of Indian consumers are not consciously aware of their rights.

Producers advertise their products, not with a view to serve the public, but with a view to dispose of their dead products at a good profit.

Many consumers are ignorant and uneducated and in such situations, the marketer exploits the consumer. There are many such cases in India.

Consumers become easy victims, in the absence of information, and buy sub-standard and defective products.

Misleading advertisements, free gifts, and similar promotional methods, may confuse consumers to make the right decision.

A single product may be available in various brands, trade mark, quality, grade and it becomes difficult for the consumer to make the right choice.

Know Your Superpower

CONSUMER IS THE KING․

“The consumer is king”. This phrase reflects the importance of consumers in every business. A "king" does not want to be commanded. It is who commands. The same goes for the consumers; the marketers cannot command the consumers to buy their goods and services.

Marketers are not doing a favor to a consumer by giving them an opportunity. Consumer is doing us a favor by allowing us to serve him. The consumer behaves like a king or queen in choosing out of those goods with his given money income.
